Product Essentials is an upskilling experience — not a rerun of new-hire Day 1, and not a feature tour. It's designed to strengthen how learners use Thumbtack product knowledge in real conversations. This version is being piloted with Sales first.

TL;DR for Delivery: success here depends on a shift from product knowledge to product use. If the live gets delivered as a recap, learners may leave feeling like they "attended training" but not noticeably more capable.

What it's for

Help learners connect product pieces, explain them clearly, and use product understanding to guide better judgment in real conversations.

For Sales: ROI conversations, weekly budget framing, visibility vs. being chosen, stronger discovery, sharper coaching on what affects pro success.

What it is not

  • A repeat of Day 1 product training
  • A lecture-heavy product overview
  • A feature memorization exercise
  • A standalone Sales methodology course
  • A replacement for live coaching, QA, or manager reinforcement

How it's built

Most of the async courses come from Product Day 1. Several pieces that were previously live in Day 1 have been converted to async courses here, since these learners are already on phones and can't be pulled away as often as new hires.

That leaves one live session in Product Essentials: Product in Practice. Calibrate it for application, not first exposure — learners bring product foundation from the async.

Product in Practice is the live application session inside Product Essentials. It's built around coaching and analysis — not lecture. The session helps learners use product knowledge in higher-value conversations, with a Product SME on hand for the harder edge-case questions.

The session feels like: "Let's slow down this moment and look at what a stronger move would be." Not: "Let me reteach all the content you should have already learned."

What the session is

For Sales, focuses on how product understanding shows up in:

  • Weekly budget hesitation
  • ROI pushback
  • Preventable late-stage objections
  • Stronger consultative reasoning
  • Earlier surfacing of what's shaping the pro's concern

What it is not

  • A basic product recap
  • A read-through of course content
  • A slide lecture
  • A generic discussion of "best practices"
  • A session where the trainer gives the answer too early
SME

Product SME role

The SME is part of the design — not optional background support. They're there to handle the harder questions that come up once learners try to apply the content.

  • Unexpected product behavior
  • Hard-to-explain customer or pro experiences
  • Edge cases that came up on live calls

Delivery's job for the SME

  • Make sure the SME is invited and has access
  • Frame the SME segment clearly for learners
  • Keep questions specific and tied to real work
  • Route higher-complexity questions to the SME — don't guess

The Slack layer is part of the learning experience — not a separate add-on. Review the prompts and learner responses before the live, then use them to shape the What We Saw This Week section of the deck.

Sample prompts

Scenario · Expectations
A pro says: "I thought once I signed up, I'd automatically start getting a steady stream of jobs."
Reinforces marketplace foundation and expectation-setting.
Scenario · Visibility vs. hire
A pro says: "Customers are seeing my profile, so why aren't they hiring me?"
Builds fluency around customer behavior, trust, and progression.
Scenario · Budget
A pro says: "I don't know if I want to commit to that weekly budget right now."
Targets one of the clearest Sales gaps. Stronger language, no soft asks.
Scenario · ROI
A pro says: "Why should I put that much in if I don't know what I'll get back?"
Targets ROI confidence — credible, consultative framing.
Observation Challenge
A pro reached out via Opportunities but the customer couldn't see their phone number. "What's the point?"
Concrete product-behavior mystery using the CX app simulator.

Activity types

  • Written scenario response — practice language in realistic moments
  • Trivia / myth-or-reality — light fluency check with reasoning
  • Product observation challenge — solve a product-behavior mystery

How to use responses in the live

Before facilitating, pull from the week's responses:

  • One standout response worth highlighting
  • One common miss worth coaching against
  • One recurring question worth surfacing live

Where it lands

Enter all three into the What We Saw This Week deck section before the session.

The Delivery Team's job is not just to "present" Product Essentials. The role is to preserve the design intent — and the live session is where most of that intent succeeds or breaks down.

In the live, do

  • Open clearly and efficiently
  • Assume product foundation is already in place
  • Use slides as prompts for thinking, not lecture
  • Coach toward better diagnosis, not just better wording
  • Stay grounded in what the pro is really reacting to
  • Pull in the SME for higher-complexity product questions
  • Review Slack responses before the live
  • Enter Slack feedback into What We Saw This Week
  • Complete the Coda speaker-notes doc before delivery

Don't

  • Reteach the full product curriculum
  • Turn the live into a lecture
  • Rush to the "best answer" before learners think
  • Drift into unrelated objection handling or generic Sales talk
  • Flatten coaching into script rewrites only
  • Guess on edge cases when the SME should be brought in
  • Treat the Slack layer as separate from the live
  • Leave the speaker-notes Coda doc incomplete

Delivery matters here because the success of this training depends on a shift — from product knowledge to product use. Facilitated as designed, learners leave sharpened. Facilitated as a recap, they don't.

If facilitated as designed

Learners should leave with:

  • Clearer judgment
  • Stronger language
  • Better recognition of what's really happening in the moment
  • Better ability to apply product understanding in their role

Tone for tenured learners

Especially important: tenured learners should feel sharpened, not talked down to. The session should respect what they already bring to the work.
